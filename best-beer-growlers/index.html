Quick answer

The Hydro Flask 64 oz Standard Mouth Growler is our top pick because it keeps beer ice-cold for hours and is built to last through years of trips to the taproom.

The YETI Rambler is the premium choice for those who want maximum durability and insulation, while the Tera Pora glass growler is the go-to for budget-minded drinkers who don’t need insulation.

A great beer growler bridges the gap between the taproom and your glass, keeping your favorite draft fresh and carbonated on the way home. Whether you are a weekly growler-filler or an occasional home-brew transporter, the right vessel depends on how far you travel, how long you need the beer to stay cold, and your tolerance for glass versus stainless steel. To choose, start with insulation: vacuum-insulated stainless steel models maintain temperature for a full day or more, while glass growlers offer purity of taste at a lower price but are fragile. Capacity is almost always 64 ounces (a half-gallon), but mouth size matters for easy pouring and cleaning. Build quality, lid design, and weight also factor in.
