Quick answer

The Tera Pora 64 oz glass growler is our top budget pick because it combines classic looks, a reliable swing-top seal, and a price that won’t dent your wallet.

Grab the Iron Flask if you need a rugged, temperature-holding stainless steel option, and choose the Libbey Classic for a timeless glass design with a comfortable flip cap.

A beer growler is one of the simplest ways to bring fresh draft beer home from your local brewery or taproom. You don’t need a kegerator or a fancy CO₂ setup, just a clean bottle, a tight seal, and a plan to drink it within a day or two. For the budget-minded drinker, the key is finding a growler that seals well, cleans easily, and won’t break the bank. Glass growlers let you see the beer and are easy to sanitize, but they’re fragile and offer zero insulation. Stainless steel models keep beer cold for hours and survive tumbles, but they’re heavier and hide the brew inside. We researched owner feedback and published reviews to pick three reliable options that prove you don’t need to spend a lot to get a solid growler.
